The Buildana method

A five-phase build process designed to eliminate late surprises.

Buildana’s process borrows from the way developers control risk: feasibility before emotion, decisions at the right time, and clear handoffs between design, approvals and construction.

See the five phases Start with feasibility
Process promise:

You will always know what decision is coming next, why it matters, and what it does to timing and budget. If something changes, it will be explained early.

Decision promptsClear milestonesUpfront trade-offs

Five phases

Each phase has a purpose, and we make that purpose obvious.

When clients understand the purpose of each phase, they make sharper decisions. That’s why we teach the process as we run it.

01

Feasibility

Site review, design-fit mapping, and early identification of cost drivers such as slope, service positions and overlay requirements.

02

Design

We align the layout, façade and inclusion tier to your site and budget, then show upgrade pathways that preserve buildability.

03

Prepare

Engineering, energy reports, approvals and documentation run as a coordinated sprint so you reach site with fewer open variables.

04

Build

Construction is led by experienced site leadership with milestone reporting that makes sense to both families and developers.

05

Handover+

Defects management, warranty education and ongoing support treated like part of the product experience — not a postscript.

Process outcomes

What this method is designed to improve.

The process is not about ticking boxes — it’s about improving the outcomes clients feel and see.

Stronger decisions early

Feasibility before design attachment reduces expensive late pivots.

Cleaner approvals rhythm

Coordinated documentation reduces stalls between consultants and certifiers.

Lower variation exposure

Site notes and upgrade pathways are clarified before contract, not after.

Higher build confidence

Milestone reporting keeps clients informed and reduces build-stage anxiety.

Better street outcomes

Façade decisions are made with covenant and context in mind.

Developer-grade clarity

The process holds up under scrutiny from both families and investors.
